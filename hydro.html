Hydro Energy

What is Hydro Energy?

Hydro power is a form of renewable energy that is generated by the force of moving water, such as a river or waterfall. Hydro power plants use the energy of the moving water to generate electricity. The first hydro power plant was built in the United States in 1882 in Appleton, Wisconsin.

Hydro power has a long history. The ancient Greeks were the first to use it, harnessing the power of water wheels to grind grain and pump water. In the late 1700s, the French inventor Bernard Forest de Belidor wrote a book that outlined the principles of hydro power and its potential uses. In the 1800s, many nations began to construct hydro-electric dams and turbines to generate electricity.

How is Hyrdro Energy Renewable

Today, hydro power is the most widely used form of renewable energy, providing about 20% of the world’s total electricity. It is a clean, reliable form of energy that does not produce carbon dioxide or other pollutants. Hydro power is an important part of many countries’ energy mix, and its use is expected to grow in the coming years.

How much hydro energy is being used presently?

Currently, hydro electricity is being used to generate around 16% of the world’s total electricity. It is estimated that hydro power plants around the world have a combined installed capacity of around 1,000 gigawatts, which is enough to power around 800 million homes. Hydro power is the most widely used form of renewable energy, providing a clean and reliable source of energy for many countries.

In many countries, hydroelectric power is being replaced by other forms of renewable energy, such as wind and solar power. These forms of energy have become increasingly popular due to their affordability, cleanliness, and scalability. Wind and solar energy have also become more cost-competitive in recent years, making them more attractive alternatives to hydroelectric power.
