What is Solar Energy?

The term "solar energy" describes energy that comes from the sun's rays. It is a renewable energy source that is the most prevalent and easily accessible type of energy on earth. Solar panels are used to capture solar energy because they turn sunlight into usable electricity for buildings and houses. Moreover, solar energy can be used for cooking, heating water and air, and other tasks that call for heat or power. Using solar energy can assist in reducing the dependency on fossil fuels, which are limited resources and have detrimental effects on the environment.

Solar energy don't just refers to the use of solar panels to generate electrical it can also be in the form of heat energy. Solar energy can be used to heat water, air, and other things.

Solar energy has been used for centuries in various forms. For example, it was used by the Ancient Greeks to heat homes, or the Ancient Chinese to make fire. In the 19th century, solar energy was harnessed to produce electricity. This was done by using the photovoltaic effect, which is the conversion of sunlight into electricity.

How is Solar Energy Renewable

Solar energy is a renewable energy source, meaning it will never run out. This is because the sun will not run out of energy anytime soon. Solar energy is also a clean energy source because it does not create any pollution when it is being used.
